                <hr class="underline">
                <p>A popular takoyaki brand in the Philippines, "Taragis Takoyaki" was founded with the goal of introducing real Japanese street food to the neighborhood. Carl Quion founded the company with the intention of presenting takoyaki in a way that would appeal to Filipinos. The concept for this brand was fueled by the founder's passion for entrepreneurship and his love of Japanese food. Taragis was founded by Filipino entrepreneur Carl Quion, and since launching his business in October 2020, he has gained recognition as an entrepreneur, Carl Romulo Quion is his real name, and he was born in Caloocan, Manila, on April 27, 1991. In order to connect with the local community, the name "Taragis," a colloquial Filipino expression, was selected.</p>
                <p>When the founder of "Taragis Takoyaki" visited Japan, they were captivated by the street food culture there, especially takoyaki. Their goal was to make it Filipino-friendly while keeping its Japanese heritage, so they incorporated regional flavors like cheese and bacon, which became well-liked right away. The company distinguished itself from other food stalls thanks to their entrepreneurial spirit and ambition to provide the community with something special. From a single stand, "Taragis Takoyaki" grew over time to several locations in both Metro Manila and other major cities. Food fusion and successful entrepreneurship are symbolized by the brand, which has become a mainstay at food bazaars due to its innovative menu and dedication to quality.</p>
